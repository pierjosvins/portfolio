Recent works

Cervical Cancer Classification Using Deep Learning Techniques

Cervical cancer is the fourth most prevalent female malignancy in terms of incidence and mortality. According to GLOBOCAN 2020, an estimated 604,000 new cases and 342,000 related deaths occurred overall. In this study, we develop and evaluate the performances of seven of the recent Deep Learning techniques for Cervical Cancer pathology classification over two datasets: LBC Pap Smear and CESC.

RealTime Face Mask Detection For Preventing the Spread of COVID-19

COVID-19 has rapidly affected our day-to-day life disrupting the world movements insofar as wearing a protective face mask has become a new normal. Developing the face mask detector becomes very crucial in this case to help global society. This paper presents a CNN approach to achieve this purpose using Deep Learning techniques.

Read Paper

Obesity Levels Classification Using Machine Learning

The purpose of this project is to estimate the level of obesity of an individual according to his eating habits and his physical condition. Like any other chronic disease, obesity becomes irreversible once it sets in. So, preventing its development is therefore essential if we want to stop this global epidemic. In this perspective, we will understand the different causes of this disease and implement an intelligent system that classifies obesity levels.
